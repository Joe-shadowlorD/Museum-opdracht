History of RijksMuseum

It started On November 19, 1798, the government decided to set up a national museum following the French example. A 'prestige project' to stimulate the love for the country but also to preserve important objects. On May 31, 1800, this National Art Gallery opened its doors for the first time in Huis Ten Bosch in The Hague. With over 200 paintings and historical objects, partly from the stadholder's collections and partly from (discontinued) national institutions, such as the VOC. the first purchase, The Swan by Jan Asselijn, cost 100 guilders and is still one of the highlights of the Rijksmuseum.
